Personalized Medicine

The aim of the project

  • Extract and preprocess biomedical data of cardiovascular patients from Surgut cardioclinic.
  • Develop ML models for prognostics of patients health status in regard to cardiovascular disease complications.
  • Determine minimum clinical features input for convenient practical diagnostics.
  • Publish recommendations for Russian cardiologists community.

Abstract

Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the leading cause of death worldwide, causing up to 18 million deaths annually according to WHO. CVD is mainly manifested in the form of myocardial infarction and heart failure. Reducing premature mortality from CVD is possible by identifying population at highest risk and ensuring that it receives appropriate treatment. The most important condition for the provision of appropriate treatment and counseling is the identification of a clinical or biochemical sign, the value of which leads to an increased risk factor. The critical values of such features can vary from population to population, so it is of particular importance to identify a group of features for the Russian population that are better able to predict an increased risk, as well as to identify their critical values.

In this work a prognostic model was developed using ML methods on the native Russian population: analysis of medical data of patients (hospitalized with CVD) to predict future complications (the need for revascularization, death, and a combined negative outcome) for a period of 1 to 10 years. Key clinical and biochemical parameters leading to increased risk in patients with CVD were determined. Predicted risks were compared to the predictions made by previously developed models.
