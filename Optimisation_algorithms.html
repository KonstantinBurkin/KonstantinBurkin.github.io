1. Gradient descent

Description of algorithm:
This iterative optimization algorithm is used for finding a local minimum of a differentiable function by taking repeated steps in the opposite direction of the gradient.
$$x_{n+1} = x_n - α * \nabla F(x_n)$$
The task:
How many iterations does the gradient descent method with a constant step of \(α = 10^{-4} \) take to achieve an accuracy in gradient norm less than \(10^{-4} \) ?

Answer: 88 iterations.


2. Heavy ball method

Description of algorithm:
This algorithm, also known as Polyak’s momentum, combines the gradient descent with a history of the previous step to accelerate the convergence of the algorithm.
$$x_{n+1} = x_n - α * \nabla F(x_n) + β(x_n-x_{n-1})$$

The task:
How many iterations does the heavy ball method with a constant step of \(α = 10^{-4} \) and coefficient \(β = 0.5 \) take to achieve an accuracy in the gradient norm less than \(10^{-4} \) ?

Answer: 47 iterations.


3. Accelerated gradient descent

Description of algorithm:
This algorithm, also known as Nesterov Momentum, is an extension to the gradient descent optimization algorithm. It makes the gradient descent with reference to the approximate future position.
$$y_0 = x_0 $$$$x_{n+1} = y_{n} - α * \nabla F(y_n) $$$$y_{n+1} = x_{n+1} + β(x_{n+1}-x_n)$$
The task:
How many iterations does the accelerated gradient method with a constant step of \(α = 3* 10^{-4} \) take to achieve an accuracy in gradient norm less than \(10^{-4}\) ?

Answer: 33 iterations.


4. Newton method

Description of algorithm:
Newton's method is an iterative method for finding a local minimum of a twice-differentiable function. At each iteration, it amounts to the fitting of a parabola to the graph of \(f(x)\).
$$x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac {F^{'}(x_n)}{ F^{''}(x_n)}$$
The task:
After how many iterations does Newton's method with a constant step of \(α = 10^{-1} \) begins to stagnate, that is, the gradient norm stops changing? In your answer, indicate the number of iteration k such that the value of the gradient norm at the next iteration k+1 remains the same.
What rate of convergence of Newton's method is observed in the norm of the gradient?

Answer: 144 iterations.
Linear rate of convergence is observed.
